about

Dream

hobbies

>manga

I read manga since I was in primary school. I still can recall the memories that I read manga in the library at lunch time at that time. "Doraemon" is the first Japanese manga series I read and the stories was engaging and funny to me. I've been collecting comics since then. I love the way how comic tells the story. Unlike novels and fictions, comic is a combination of graphics and words. It is easier for the reader to image what's actually happening. It is also a new culture and way to tell the stories with greater flexibility. It's hard to find reading a basketball stories with plain texts funny. But I guess everyone born in 80-90's should have heard of "slam dunk", which is a very famous sports-themed manga series. Nowadays, comics become part of Japanese culture, and manga artists are respected and admired by people of all ages. Manga also gained significant worldwide audience. Manga is a kind of arts to me. I always read a series more than once if I found the stories especially captivating and interesting. Artists have different drawing styles and ways to tell the story. And there are some themes which really inspire me or touch my spirit. Even now, I still read manga in my pastime to release my pressure at work and study.

games

I love play all kind of games since I was small, especially computer games. I am especially addicted to the strategic games and card games, such as chess, poker and room escape. It brings me lots of excitement and sense of achievement when I win the game. I also enjoy fps games such as "League Of Legends" and "PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds". Actually the passion for computers started because of games. When I play a game, I'd love to investigate the algorithm and how to optimize it. I am always eager to learn the latest information technologies as well as the applications in our daily lives.

music

I love classical music, jpop and electronic music. Music plays quite an important role in my life. Of all hobbies in the world,I like music most. I enjoy listening to music in everywhere. Especially when I feel stressed or down, I always relax myself by listening to music. I prefer piano songs when I want to focus on study or work. And if I am in a good mood, I'd love to listen to rock music or songs with fast tempo.

I always regret for giving up learning musical instruments in my childhood. When I was in primary one , my mom took me to the violin class but I eventually gave up because of difficulties. And I finally determined to learn a musical instrument in my university life. I chose piano because I love how it sounds and it is a great instrument for solo play. The instrument allows you to play both the melody and the accompaniment at the same time. It is also wide in tonal range and volume, making it easier to play songs in different styles.

I practiced quite hard during my university study. I bought a kawai piano and took private piano lessons at my teacher's home. Owing to my persistence to practice hard, my progress is fairly fast. My teacher then encouraged me to take grade 5 ABRSM piano examination after one year of study. I practice even harder at that time and fortunately I obtained a distinction. In fact I felt very stressed and nervous during the examination and my hands sweat all the time. In year 3, my teacher invited me to participate in the X'mas Student Concert and I got 2nd prize in my age group. It was the first time I played the grand piano in front of so many audience, including the students and their parents. I still felt quite stressed, my hands kept sweating and my heart beated very fast. I almost forgot the notes and played by muscle memories. The tempo goes faster than normal speed but it is basically smooth.

Time flies like an arrow. My fresh graduate position as an assistant engineer in a building service company drived me very busy and eventually I gave up continuing to play the piano. I wish one day I can pick it up again when I am not that busy. But in the coming few years I may focus more on my career for a better living standard.
